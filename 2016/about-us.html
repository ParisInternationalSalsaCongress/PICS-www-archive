ABOUT US

5 years from now, I started to become obsessed by the idea of organizing a Salsa congress in the very heart of Paris.

In my humble opinion, Paris is where History, Art, Culture, Architecture and of course Dance meet in the most harmonious and natural way. Anyone who has visited Paris knows that this city never stops surprising by her unique beauty and her charm.

The concept matured in my mind years after years and I can’t recall the number of times I talked about it with my dear salsa buddies who will surely recognize themselves.

To me, not having our own major salsa congress where French and international salseros would meet to dance together on the same dance floor every year was simply not acceptable, and I was thinking like CRAZY how to make it happen..

Now PISC is finally becoming a reality and I feel so happy and so excited about it!

Being a Salsa performer and teacher for more than 15 years, I had and I still have now the privilege to travel to many countries and to create strong bounds with amazing people from the salsa and kizomba world. We are all part of a big family and I am already so grateful to all my dancer friends who responded so quickly and positively to my call, and also for the huge enthusiasm everyone is showing after hearing the news.

Of course, this project cannot be put together alone. I am very lucky to have together with me a trustful and talented team of Salsa addicts who put all their heart and passion to help me in this adventure.

Some of you might know that in “my previous life” I have been practicing intensively a Martial Art called Karate-do. Karate is all about humility, selflessness, honesty and perseverance and I wish to commit here to everyone that my team and I, driven by those key values, will do everything in our power to make sure you have the most amazing experience at the first edition of the PISC.

This congress is not possible without you, we need you, we need your support that is why it is not about me, but “About us”.

I can’t wait for the Congress to start to welcome you all my friends.

Management TEAM

Mouaze Konaté

Cf. artist section

Mouaze is a fundamental element in the organisation of the Paris International Salsa Congress. Demanding, he sets the ambition to make Paris a recognized reference within the salsa congress community. He is also the light that motivates and guides the PISC organisation team.

Last of all, he brings his sense of detail and esthetic on several areas, from artist management to communication.

ELEONORA JAUK

Cf. artist section

Ella is definitely a multi-tasking machine within the PISC organisation.

From artist booking, to ticket management, to workshop planning, she makes sure that there is no gap in the preparation of this event which will gather dancers from the 5 continents.

Freddy Bellon

Native from Reunion Island , Freddy moved to Paris when he was 18. He discovered salsa in 2007 and decided to take it to the next level after watching Terry & Cecile perform one of their early shows but his encounter with Mouaze was the real revelation. Since he joined Mouaze School 5 years ago, first as a student then as a dancer and now a teacher, his passion for On2 New York style salsa never stopped.

Former consultant now working in finance, Freddy puts his finance and project management skills at the service of the PISC. He is in charge of sales, treasury as well as the global project oversight.

Wafa Abdelmoula

Born in Tunis, Wafa joins the National Ballet of Tunis at the age of 5. She will dance there for 8 years, following a very diverse training in classical, contemporary and oriental.

In Paris since 2010, Wafa continues her dance training in Hip Hop, Ragga Dancehall and salsa. She is lucky enough to learn alongside world famous teachers such as the Maestro Mouaze Konaté

Thanks to her skills in project management, in communication and her passion for dancing, Wafa joined the adventure and is in charge of the Communication department.

Fanny Frosini

Cf. artist section

Originally in charge of the Mouaze School Newsletter, Fanny is in charge of all physical and virtual promotion for the PISC. She works alongside Wafa for the Communication department

Muriel Dimiccoli

French with an Italian flavor, Muriel loves to be on stage!

She began learning salsa at the end of her studies and chose to perfect her technique at the Mouaze School, under Mouaze and Ella's guidance, where she rapidly becomes part of the Mouaze Dancers dance team in 2013.

She has had the opportunity to perform several times in France and abroad with the team: Paris, Bordeaux, Copenhagen, Rabat, Pisa...

A former management consultant now in charge of Client Relationship Management in the consumer goods industry, Muriel puts her skills in human resources management at the service of the PISC.

She is in charge of managing our team of helpful volunteers who will do everything to make this congress an unforgettable experience for you.

Eleonor Morales

Born in the Balearic islands, Léo grew up in Paris and took classical and modern jazz lessons before discovering the salsa world.

In 2009, she falls in love with the NY Style, first with Sève, then with Mouaze who will become her mentor.

In Parallel, she recently started to learn the lift and tricks technique linked to accro yoga practice and also developed a liking for the samba rythms.

Expert in the organisation of tailor made trips, Léo is in charge of the artists from arrival to departure. She is our Artist relationship coordinator.

Yoann Dambrun

Discreet but with a knack for the stage, Yoann entered the world of salsa in 2005. He started to learn cuban style in Rennes when he was still a student and keeps practicing the On2 style in Paris under Mouaze Konaté's guidance today. Portoricans rythms delight him, make him stamp his feet, shake his head until body and mind exult... on the dancefloor!

Yoann brings his practical mind to the team in charge of organizing the congress. Amongst other things, he is in charge of organizing and preparing the congress center. He will make sure that each and everyone of you are welcomed in the best conditions for a memorable experience.

Julien Sarton

Julien has been dancing Hip Hop and salsa for 12 years now. He trained himself at the Mouaze School for 3 years before leaving for new adventures in Argentina where he got inspired by new influences, among which Rumba and Afro Cuban.

Back in Paris since 2014, he keeps learning alongside the best salsa teachers, amongst which the famous Mouaze Konaté.

Julien puts his entrepreneurial spirit (he is the founder of Fit Factory, a sport marketing agency) and his talent as event organizer at the service of the PISC. Amongst other things, he works for the Corporate Sponsorship department.

Stéphane Coué

Native from the west indies, Stéphane is a dance addict since 2003. Cuban salsa, then tango for 6 years, he has since then become a mambo enthusiast. Living in Paris for 2 years now, he is one of the soldier of the Mouaze School where he keeps learning and developping his technique ("Again and Again"). In parallel, he discovered Afro Cuban, which makes him think that he still has a long way to go before mastering all these different rythms.

Specialised in the new information and communication technologies, Stéphane put his IT skills at the service of the congress. He works for the Web and Digital department.

Yannick

Yannick started learning portorican salsa in 2009 in Lille. He picked interest in the NY style after attending the Antwerp Diamond Salsa Congress where had the opportunity to attend a workshop with the Maestro Mouaze Konaté. Thanks to a career opportunity he moves to Paris in 2012 and starts taking classes at Mouaze School in order to learn the style that inspired him so. He was honored to join the Mouaze dancers in 2014.

Yannick is the handyman of the PISC organisation. He brings his support in all urgent matters and helps us promote the PISC in all the parisian parties.
